# Why LMS Systems Fail Care Training

## The Core Problem

Most learning management systems were built for content delivery.

Care training requires behavior change.

That is the mismatch.

A learner can finish a video, pass a quiz, and still be unprepared for a real child in distress.

---

# Care Work Is Situational

Neurodivergence care requires moment-by-moment judgment.

A learner needs to know:

- when to speak
- when to pause
- how to reduce pressure
- how to observe triggers
- how to reinforce behavior
- how to avoid escalation
- how to document what happened

This cannot be trained properly through passive content alone.

---

# Completion Is Not Competency

Traditional LMS systems optimize for:

- module completion
- video progress
- quiz scores
- certificates

But care work needs evidence of:

- calm response
- safety awareness
- observation quality
- reinforcement planning
- communication clarity
- supervision readiness

The real question is not:

“Did the learner finish the module?”

The real question is:

“Can the learner respond well in a difficult situation?”

---

# Simulations Create Practice Loops

Simulation-based learning creates a different loop:

1. learner sees a realistic case
2. learner responds
3. system evaluates competency
4. learner receives coaching
5. learner retries
6. system tracks improvement

This turns training into practice.

---

# The Missing Layer

Most LMS systems do not create:

- competency graphs
- behavioral simulation records
- adaptive remediation
- AI supervision notes
- readiness prediction
- employer trust signals

CareQuest exists to explore that missing layer.

---

# The Thesis

Care training should not be content-first.

It should be simulation-first, competency-first, and progression-first.
