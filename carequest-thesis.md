# CareQuest Thesis

## The Problem

Neurodivergence care is growing globally, but the workforce required to support it is not scaling fast enough.

The shortage is visible across:

- autism support
- ADHD support
- behavioral therapy
- shadow teaching
- special education
- occupational therapy support
- speech and language support

Demand exists, but trained supply is thin.

## The Insight

Most companies are trying to solve access to therapy.

But access to therapy depends on the availability of trained human workers.

The deeper bottleneck is not discovery.

It is workforce creation.

## The Starting Point

CareQuest begins with ABAT/RBT learners because certification pathways are measurable, practical, and connected to job-readiness.

The first outcome is simple:

Can we help more learners finish and become certification-ready?

## The Product Direction

CareQuest combines:

- gamified progression
- AI simulations
- competency scoring
- supervisor-style feedback
- readiness prediction
- workforce profiles

## The Long-Term Bet

The future of care work will need infrastructure similar to what software engineering got through coding platforms, GitHub, bootcamps, and professional networks.

Care workers need their own version of:

- learning infrastructure
- simulation infrastructure
- verified skill profiles
- competency graphs
- career progression systems

CareQuest is exploring that layer.
